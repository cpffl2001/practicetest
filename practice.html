Part A — Data Representation
1 Pictograph — sodas per day
Using the key (each symbol = 2 sodas), how many total sodas did Jamil drink that week?
Count symbols: Mon(3) + Tue(5) + Wed(2) + Thu(1) + Fri(3) = 14 symbols × 2 = 28 sodas.
2 Dot Plot — hours reading per week
Data: 2, 3, 1, 2, 4, 2, 3, 1, 2, 3, 2, 4. What are the mode and range of this data set?
The tallest stack is at 2 hours (5 dots) → Mode = 2. Range = 4 − 1 = 3.

The bar graph shows points scored by soccer teams on Saturday. How many total points did the Panthers and Rockets score combined?

Part B — Measures of Central Tendency
8 Bar Chart — hurricanes per decade
The bar chart shows hurricanes affecting the US by decade. What is the mean (average) number of hurricanes per decade?

Part C — Probability & Venn Diagrams
9 Simple Probability — marble bag
A bag contains 9 red, 2 blue, and 5 green marbles. What is the probability of choosing a blue marble at random?
Total = 9 + 2 + 5 = 16 marbles. P(blue) = 2/16 = 1/8.
10 Complement Rule — marble bag
A bag has 7 red, 4 blue, and 1 green marble (12 total). What is the probability of choosing a marble that is NOT blue?
P(blue) = 4/12 = 1/3. Complement: P(NOT blue) = 1 − 1/3 = 2/3.
11 Venn Diagram — dogs & fenced yards (conditional probability)
The Venn diagram shows a 100-person survey about dog ownership and fenced yards. Given that a person's yard IS fenced (35 people total), what is the probability they also own a dog?
Fenced people = 15 + 20 = 35. Of those, 15 own a dog. P(dog | fenced) = 15/35 = 3/7.
12 Venn Diagram — brown hair & glasses
The Venn diagram shows data for 26 people. How many people have brown hair AND glasses? What fraction of all 26 people is that?
The overlapping center region = 4 people. Fraction = 4/26 = 2/13.
13 Three-Circle Venn Diagram — student activities
The Venn diagram shows which activities students participate in. How many students are in all three activities (Sports AND Music AND Art)?
The center region where ALL three circles overlap shows 2. That is the count for all three activities.
14 Independent Events — two dice, sum = 7
Two fair six-sided dice are rolled. The sample space grid shows all 36 outcomes. The blue cells show outcomes where the sum equals 7. What is P(sum = 7)?
6 outcomes sum to 7 out of 36 total. P = 6/36 = 1/6.
15 Dependent Events — cards without replacement
From a standard 52-card deck, a face card (J, Q, or K) is drawn first and set aside. Then a second card is drawn. What is the probability the second card is an ace?
P(face) = 12/52 = 3/13. After removing it, 51 cards remain with 4 aces. P(ace) = 4/51. P(both) = 3/13 × 4/51 = 12/663 = 4/221.
